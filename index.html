How pilots work

A managed content loop first. A software platform as the repeatable pieces harden.

Early pilots are deliberately hands-on: we map the store, prepare structured briefs, generate content options, keep approval controlled and turn each run into product learning.

01

Map the store

Products, audience, current priorities, stock context, blocked topics and brand rules are captured before the first run.

02

Choose safe formats

Product spotlights, category guides, campaign offers, evergreen tips or news-style posts are selected based on what the store can support.

03

Generate draft sets

The app helps prepare carousel outlines, captions, CTAs and image direction from a structured brief, with manual QA during pilots.

04

Approve before output

Owners review the important decisions before anything is published or prepared for a campaign.

05

Record the learning

Each run creates a short evidence log so the next batch is sharper and the future platform is built on real operating data.

Why this matters

Most e-commerce stores do not need more tools. They need execution.

Agencies are expensive and slow.

Routine marketing work should not cost thousands a month before a store even knows what angle will work.

AI content without inventory context is shallow.

The product direction is to connect stock, margins and campaign opportunities so content follows business reality.

Pilots should create momentum, not extra work.

Masthead is designed to give owners a working content and campaign loop without adding another dashboard they have to manage every day.
